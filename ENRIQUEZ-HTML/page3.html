Bentley Bacalar

The Bentley Bacalar is a rare and ultra-luxurious open-top grand tourer designed by Bentley’s bespoke division, Mulliner. Named after Lake Bacalar in Mexico’s Yucatán Peninsula, the Bacalar is inspired by natural beauty and embodies exclusivity, luxury, and performance. With only 12 units produced, the Bacalar is one of the rarest modern Bentleys ever made. Here’s an in-depth look at its features and specifications:

1. Design and Exterior

Body Style:

The Bacalar is a two-seater, open-top roadster, designed to evoke the spirit of classic Bentley models and handcrafted luxury.

Exterior Material:

The Bacalar’s body panels are made from lightweight aluminum and carbon fiber, with no shared panels with any other Bentley model. The unique material selection contributes to both the vehicle's performance and exclusivity.

Paint Options:

Each Bacalar can be custom-painted, with Bentley offering unique paints that use rice husk ash to create sustainable metallic finishes.

Aerodynamics:

The design is sculpted for both elegance and stability, with flowing lines that contribute to its aerodynamic efficiency. The muscular rear haunches and signature Bentley grille showcase the brand’s characteristic design language.

Lights:

Unique round headlights with LED matrix technology and rear taillights that seamlessly integrate with the rear deck give the Bacalar a distinctive look.

2. Engine and Performance

Engine:

The Bacalar is powered by Bentley’s 6.0-liter W12 engine, a hallmark of Bentley’s power and performance.

Horsepower:

It delivers 650 horsepower and 667 lb-ft of torque.

Top Speed:

The Bacalar has a top speed of around 200 mph (322 km/h), delivering the performance expected from a high-end Bentley.

Acceleration:

It can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph (0 to 97 km/h) in approximately 3.5 seconds.

Transmission:

The car features an 8-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission, which provides smooth, responsive shifts suitable for grand touring and spirited driving.

3. Interior and Craftsmanship

Seating:

Unlike most Bentleys, the Bacalar is a two-seater, making it a truly personal experience. The seats are crafted from sustainable, natural materials, with intricate detailing and a unique color palette.

Sustainable Materials:

The Bacalar features wood trim crafted from 5,000-year-old, responsibly sourced Riverwood, a sustainable and unique wood exclusive to this model. Other materials include natural wool and leather sourced through sustainable practices.

Customization:

Each Bacalar is uniquely tailored to the owner’s specifications, allowing full customization of colors, materials, and detailing. From the color of the stitching to the finish of the metals, each element can be personalized.

Technology:

A digital display cluster and infotainment system with an elegant interface provide modern functionality. The controls are subtly integrated to retain a sense of classic Bentley luxury.

4. Chassis and Suspension

Chassis:

The Bacalar shares some structural components with the Bentley Continental GT but has been extensively reworked to deliver a more focused, sporty experience.

Suspension:

The Bacalar uses an adaptive air suspension system, providing a balance between comfort and performance. The system adjusts damping based on driving conditions and can raise or lower the car for clearance or performance as needed.

All-Wheel Drive:

It features an advanced all-wheel-drive system, which primarily drives the rear wheels for a sportier feel but can also send power to the front wheels when additional traction is needed.

5. Wheels and Tires

Wheels:

The Bacalar rides on unique 22-inch alloy wheels with a design inspired by the Bentley EXP 100 GT concept car.

Tires:

It is equipped with high-performance Pirelli P Zero tires specifically chosen to enhance handling, stability, and comfort.

6. Production and Price

Limited Production:

Only 12 units of the Bentley Bacalar were produced, each made to the exact specifications of its owner. This exclusivity ensures the Bacalar’s status as a collector’s piece.

Price:

The Bacalar starts at around $2 million USD, though the final price varies significantly based on customizations and bespoke options.

7. Inspiration and Design Language

Conceptual Influence:

The Bacalar’s design is inspired by the Bentley EXP 100 GT concept, which was created to celebrate Bentley’s 100th anniversary. It brings many of the concept’s futuristic elements to life while emphasizing craftsmanship.

Eco-Conscious Design:

Bentley’s choice of sustainable materials like 5,000-year-old Riverwood and the use of environmentally-friendly paints highlights the brand’s commitment to sustainability in luxury.

8. Unique Aspects

Handcrafted Exclusivity:

The Bacalar’s construction is entirely handcrafted, with each unit representing hundreds of hours of artisanal labor.

Open-Top Luxury:

Unlike most Bentleys, the Bacalar is an open-top roadster with no convertible roof option, making it ideal for grand touring in fair weather.

Personalization:

Mulliner, Bentley’s bespoke division, has provided every Bacalar owner with near-limitless customization options, meaning no two Bacalars are identical.
