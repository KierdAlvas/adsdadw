Delage D12

The Delage D12 is a hybrid hypercar by the revived French automaker Delage, known for its luxury and racing heritage. The D12 is designed to blend the thrill of Formula 1 performance with the sophistication and craftsmanship of luxury automotive design. With its unique configuration, high-powered hybrid system, and track-focused aerodynamics, the D12 is one of the most ambitious hypercars in the market. Here’s an in-depth look at the Delage D12:

1. Design and Exterior

Body Style:

The Delage D12 is a two-seater hypercar with a single-seat, fighter jet-inspired layout and a passenger seat positioned directly behind the driver, similar to a tandem configuration. This design is inspired by F1 and fighter jets, enhancing both performance and visual appeal.

Aerodynamics:

The D12’s body is meticulously sculpted for aerodynamics, with a sharp front end, aggressive side intakes, and a rear wing for downforce. The exterior design is track-focused, maximizing aerodynamic efficiency to improve stability and performance at high speeds.

Body Material:

The D12’s body is made from carbon fiber, keeping the weight low and the structure strong to withstand high speeds and intense cornering forces.

2. Engine and Hybrid Powertrain

Powertrain:

The Delage D12 uses a hybrid system that combines a powerful V12 engine with an electric motor.

Engine:

The naturally aspirated 7.6-liter V12 engine produces around 990 horsepower, crafted specifically for the D12. The engine is designed to provide an F1-like experience with a high-revving and unique sound profile.

Electric Motor:

The hybrid electric motor adds an additional 110 horsepower, bringing the combined output to an impressive 1,100 horsepower.

Transmission:

The D12 is equipped with an 8-speed automated manual transmission, which enables fast, precise gear shifts optimized for both road and track performance.

3. Performance and Handling

Top Speed:

The D12 has an estimated top speed of 233 mph (375 km/h), putting it on par with other hypercars in its class.

Acceleration:

It is expected to accelerate from 0 to 62 mph (0 to 100 km/h) in just 2.5 seconds, leveraging both the V12 and electric motor for rapid acceleration.

Suspension:

The D12 features a unique suspension setup inspired by Formula 1 technology. It has a pushrod suspension system with adjustable dampers to adapt to different driving conditions, offering both comfort and performance.

Drive Modes:

The D12 includes multiple driving modes to suit different environments, with track-focused settings that optimize throttle response, handling, and aerodynamics.

4. Chassis and Weight

Chassis:

The D12 features a carbon-fiber monocoque chassis, keeping the weight down while providing exceptional rigidity.

Weight:

Delage offers two versions of the D12:

D12 GT:

Weighs approximately 1,400 kg (3,086 pounds) and includes the hybrid system, making it suitable for both track and road.

D12 Club:

Weighs around 1,310 kg (2,888 pounds) and is a lighter, more track-oriented version with a smaller electric motor for improved handling and responsiveness.

Weight Distribution:

The car's design, with a central driver position and balanced weight distribution, allows for excellent handling dynamics.

5. Interior and Cockpit

Seating Layout:

The D12 features a unique tandem seating layout, similar to that of fighter jets, with the driver positioned in the center and the passenger directly behind. This setup provides a unique, race-car-like experience for both occupants.

Materials:

The interior is crafted from premium materials such as carbon fiber and Alcantara, with a minimalist and driver-focused design emphasizing performance.

Steering Wheel and Controls:

The F1-inspired steering wheel includes integrated controls for various functions, providing easy access to drive modes, performance settings, and more without taking hands off the wheel.

Digital Display:

A custom digital dashboard provides key information to the driver, such as speed, rpm, and battery status, all arranged to enhance focus during high-performance driving.

6. Hybrid and Electric Performance

Battery and Electric Range:

The D12’s hybrid system includes a compact battery that powers the electric motor, offering an electric-only mode for short distances and city driving. However, the range in electric mode is limited, as the primary focus is performance.

Energy Recovery:

The car features regenerative braking, which captures energy during deceleration to recharge the battery and enhance efficiency.

Eco Mode:

While primarily a performance car, the D12 has a mode that limits the use of the V12 engine, allowing the vehicle to operate on electric power in low-speed environments.

7. Production and Price

Limited Production:

Delage plans to produce only 30 units of the D12, making it an exclusive collector’s hypercar.

Price:

The Delage D12 starts at approximately €2 million (about $2.3 million USD), with options for customization and additional features.

8. Unique Aspects and Innovations

Formula 1 Influence:

The D12 is heavily influenced by Formula 1, with a single-seater style and advanced suspension system, providing a driving experience close to that of an F1 car.

Tandem Seating:

The fighter jet-inspired seating arrangement makes the D12 stand out from other hypercars, enhancing the car's exotic appeal and immersive experience.

Sound:

The naturally aspirated V12 engine provides an iconic sound profile, something increasingly rare in the hypercar world as brands shift to turbocharged and fully electric models.

9. Competitors

The Delage D12 competes with other high-performance hypercars like the Aston Martin Valkyrie, Mercedes-AMG Project One, and Gordon Murray T.50, each aiming to bring a Formula 1 experience to the road.
