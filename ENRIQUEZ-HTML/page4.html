SSC Tuatara

The SSC Tuatara is an American hypercar designed by SSC North America, known for its focus on record-breaking speed and advanced engineering. Named after the New Zealand reptile known for its unique DNA and fast growth rate, the Tuatara embodies innovation, speed, and precision. Here’s an in-depth look at the SSC Tuatara’s features and specifications:

1. Engine and Performance

Engine:

The SSC Tuatara is powered by a bespoke 5.9-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine.

Horsepower:

It delivers up to 1,750 horsepower when using E85 ethanol fuel and around 1,350 horsepower on 91-octane gasoline.

Torque:

The engine produces approximately 1,280 lb-ft of torque.

Top Speed:

SSC claims a top speed of over 300 mph (483 km/h). While the car has faced some controversy regarding its speed record attempts, SSC aims for the Tuatara to be the world’s fastest production car.

Acceleration:

It can reportedly accelerate from 0 to 60 mph (0 to 97 km/h) in just 2.5 seconds, showcasing the vehicle’s impressive power and response.

Transmission:

The car features a 7-speed automated manual transmission with a robotized shifting system, designed for ultra-fast gear changes.

2. Chassis and Body

Chassis:

The Tuatara’s monocoque chassis is made entirely of carbon fiber, ensuring a lightweight yet exceptionally strong structure.

Body Material:

The entire body is crafted from lightweight carbon fiber, contributing to the car’s low curb weight and aerodynamic efficiency.

Aerodynamics:

The Tuatara has an impressive drag coefficient of 0.279 Cd, which is among the lowest for a hypercar. The aerodynamic design includes active components such as an active rear wing to optimize downforce at high speeds.

Suspension:

It has a fully adjustable suspension system, with a front lift for increased clearance when needed. The suspension tuning aims to balance performance on both the track and the road.

3. Interior

Design:

The Tuatara’s interior is futuristic and driver-focused. It combines luxury with a race-ready aesthetic, featuring high-quality materials like Alcantara and carbon fiber.

Technology:

The car includes a digital instrument cluster, a central touchscreen for infotainment, and a steering wheel with integrated controls, designed to keep the driver’s focus on the road.

Seating:

The cabin is designed to accommodate two passengers, with bucket seats that provide strong lateral support during high-speed driving.

4. Weight and Dimensions

Weight:

The Tuatara has a curb weight of around 1,247 kg (2,749 pounds), achieved through the use of lightweight materials like carbon fiber and titanium.

Dimensions:

With its sleek, low-slung profile, the Tuatara is designed to maximize aerodynamic performance while maintaining a striking visual presence.

5. Production and Price

Limited Production:

SSC plans to produce only 100 units of the Tuatara, making it highly exclusive.

Price:

The starting price for the Tuatara is approximately $1.6 million USD, though customizations and additional options can increase this.

6. Performance Goals and Speed Record Attempts

Top Speed Attempts:

The Tuatara aimed to break the production car speed record, attempting a run in October 2020. While SSC initially claimed a record of 316 mph (508 km/h), the claim faced scrutiny, and SSC later acknowledged inconsistencies in the data. In subsequent attempts, the Tuatara reached 282.9 mph (455.3 km/h) during a verified run in January 2021, and SSC remains committed to reaching the 300 mph mark in future runs.

7. Key Competitors

The Tuatara competes with other hypercars pushing speed limits, including the Bugatti Chiron Super Sport 300+, Hennessey Venom F5, and Koenigsegg Jesko Absolut.

8. Unique Aspects

Aerodynamic Efficiency:

The Tuatara’s drag coefficient of 0.279 Cd is one of the lowest for a car of its class, allowing it to achieve extreme speeds with less resistance.

Engine Design:

SSC’s bespoke twin-turbo V8 is engineered specifically for high-speed stability, with a flat-plane crankshaft to reduce vibration and increase smoothness at high RPMs.

Exclusivity and Performance Heritage:

With limited production and SSC’s reputation (bolstered by the SSC Ultimate Aero’s legacy), the Tuatara stands as a symbol of American hypercar engineering.
